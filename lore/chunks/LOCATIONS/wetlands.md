## Overview

The **Wetlands Sector** (designated **Sector W** in Terminal 7-B records) comprises the southeastern marshlands of Degenora Island. Formed from the convergence of three freshwater drainage systems and the island's natural sinkhole topography, this region has become a primary habitat for swamp-adapted specimens and one of the most biodiverse zones in the documented sectors.

Unlike the contaminated ruins of the Main Facility, the Wetlands represent a space where specimens have developed their own ecological orderâ€”one that observes Frugowski from a distance but has established its own rules of engagement.

## Geography

### The Shallows (W-12 to W-18)

The outer rim of the Wetlands, where solid ground gradually surrenders to marsh. Observers from Terminal 7-B report this is where most incidental encounters occurâ€”specimens here are curious but generally non-aggressive, often content to watch from the treeline.

Notable features:
- **The Mud Flats**: Expansive stretches where the water table meets the surface. Movement is slow but possible. Specimens have learned to use the mud's suction as a defensive advantage.
- **Cypress Groves**: Ancient trees adapted to flooded conditions. Their root systems form natural corridors that specimens use as highways through the sector.

### The Deep Mire (W-19 to W-25)

Where the Wetlands become truly dangerous. The ground here is unreliableâ€”solid one day, liquid the next. Frugowski's early expeditions into this zone resulted in multiple equipment losses and one close encounter that remains partially classified.

Notable features:
- **The Sinking Pools**: Areas of liquefied substrate that can swallow equipment (and Observers) whole. Marked on updated maps with the âš ï¸ symbol.
- **Bioluminescent Zones**: Pockets where fungal colonies emit a persistent blue-green glow. The effect is visually striking but the spores can cause mild hallucinations in unprotected Observers.

### The Heart (W-26 to W-28)

The innermost Wetlands region. Few visual records exist. What Frugowski has documented suggests this is where the sector's eldest specimens congregateâ€”those who were among the first transformed and have had fifteen years to adapt to their environment.

## Inhabitants

The Wetlands are home to the **Swamp-Adapted Specimens**â€”a loose designation covering an estimated 2,000+ transformed entities who have developed traits suited to aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.

Common adaptations observed:
- **Amphibious respiration**: Many specimens can extract oxygen from water through modified skin surfaces
- **Bioluminescence**: Symbiotic bacteria colonies on several specimen types produce the sector's characteristic glow
- **Mud camouflage**: Dermal secretions that bind sediment, rendering specimens nearly invisible when motionless
