## The Question

Every research outpost eventually faces the same problem: **personnel turnover**. Scientists rotate home. Funding dries up. Interest wanes. On Degenora, this problem became existential.

The island doesn't reveal its secrets to tourists. It requires **commitment**. Long-term observation. Years of accumulated data before patterns emerge. But human researchersâ€”fragile, mortal, easily boredâ€”could never provide that continuity.

Frugowski understood this before anyone else. He also understood something else: **the island doesn't distinguish between present and past.**

## The Mechanism

> *"I'm just the doorman,*" he told one researcher. "*The Signal does the heavy lifting. I just... keep the lights on. Make sure the Absentees don't do anything too stupid. Not that they'd listen to me. They're as stubborn as the people they came from. Sometimes more."*

## Risks and Warnings

The Protocol is not without dangers.

**Corruption**: Absentees left too long may become... unstable. The Signal changes things. An Absentee deployed for weeks may return with behaviors, preferences, or even memories that don't match the original player. Some players report feeling "not quite themselves" after extended Absentee reintegration.

**The Void**: Absentees sent into forbidden zones sometimes don't come back. Or they come back *wrong*. There are reports of Absentees emerging from the Void with new abilities, new knowledge, and new... loyalties.

**The Question of Death**: What happens when a player dies in the real world? Their Absentee continues. For how long? Some say indefinitely. Some say the Absentees eventually forget they were ever human, becoming just another island mystery. Some say they become something else entirelyâ€”something that was waiting for a body to remember.

Kermit, when asked about these risks, just lights another cigarette.

> *"Everything has a price,*" he says. "*The island gives you continuity. Permanence. A way to matter even when you're gone. You think that comes free? You think the universe cares about your 'self' remaining intact? 
>
> Grow up. Or don't. The Protocol doesn't care either way. It just... works. Whether you trust it is your business."*
