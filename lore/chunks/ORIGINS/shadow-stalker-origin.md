## The Wolves That Were

Before Year 0, Degenora Island had no wolves. The original ecological surveys were clear on this point: the island's isolation and limited land area made it unsuitable for large mammalian predators. The food chain topped out with birds of prey and a species of oversized feral cat that posed minimal threat to humans.

The wolves arrived **with** the Signal. Or perhaps more accurately: the Signal **made** them from something else.

The first Shadow Stalker was documented in Year 2, eleven months after the Incident. But the process that created it began much earlier, in the space between moments, in the dark places where the Signal's broadcast seemed to... hesitate. To stutter.

The Void was not always there. The researchers of Year 0 mapped the island extensively, and Sector 7 was unremarkableâ€”a dense forest, difficult terrain, but nothing anomalous. Then, slowly, it began to **thin**. Not the trees. The space between the trees. The light. The sound.

Something was eating the world in Sector 7. And it was hungry for more.

## The Void Grows

Dr. Marcus Wei was the first to propose that the Void was not a place but a **process**. A kind of infection spreading through the fabric of reality itself, carried by the Signal but distinct from it.

In his famous (and final) paper, *"On the Nature of Void Spaces"* (Year 7), he wrote:

> *"The Signal broadcasts information. This is its natureâ€”we receive it, interpret it, are changed by it. But information can be corrupted. Signals can degrade. What we call 'the Void' may be the result of Signal degradation: areas where the broadcast has become so distorted, so filled with error and noise, that it actively **destroys** coherent information."*

> *"In the Void, things lose definition. Boundaries blur. A tree becomes a concept of a tree, then a memory of a tree, then nothing at all. Living things are not immune to this process. They are, in some ways, more vulnerableâ€”because life is, at its core, organized information. Complex patterns. And the Void... unpatterns."*

But here's the terrifying part: some things **survive** the unpatterning.

Not unchanged. Never unchanged. But recognizable. Functional. Evenâ€”improbablyâ€”enhanced.

The Shadow Stalkers are what happens when a mammalian predator encounters the Void and **doesn't** dissolve into static.

## The Pack Mind

Shadow Stalkers hunt in coordinated groups of 3-7 individuals. This is unusual for canid predators, which typically hunt in pairs or small family groups. More unusual: the coordination is **perfect**.

Dr. Wei observed a Shadow Stalker pack hunting Bog Hoppers for three consecutive nights. His notes describe movements that seemed choreographed, as if all pack members shared a single consciousness:

> *"The flankers moved before the prey reacted. Before the prey COULD react. They knew where the Bog Hopper would jump before it jumped. They anticipated strategies that should have been impossible to predict."*

> *"I recorded one instance where a Shadow Stalker paused mid-chase, turned 90 degrees, and intercepted a Bog Hopper that had changed direction. The change happened 0.4 seconds before the interception. The Stalker began turning 0.6 seconds BEFORE the Bog Hopper changed direction."*

> *"They are not predicting. They are **remembering**. Remembering futures that haven't happened yet."*

The working theory: Shadow Stalkers exist partially outside normal time. The Void's corruption has granted them access to information that hasn't been generated yetâ€”probabilities, possibilities, the branching tree of causality.

They don't just hunt. They **select** futures where their prey dies.

## Kermit's Warning

Kermit rarely speaks about the Shadow Stalkers. When he does, his usual cynicism is replaced by something that might be respect. Or fear.

> *"They're not animals,*" he said once, when a new researcher asked about them. "*Animals want to eat, to reproduce, to survive. The Stalkers want something else. Something I can't... something I don't want to understand."*

> *"The Void made them, but the Void doesn't control them. They're like... antibodies. The island's immune response. They hunt things that don't belong, things that disrupt the pattern. Bog Hoppers that get too independent. Specimens that evolve too fast. People who learn too much."*

He paused. Lit a cigarette.

> *"Dr. Wei thought he could study them. Thought he could understand what they are, where they come from. He was wrong. You don't study the Void. You survive it, or you don't. He didn't."*

Dr. Wei entered Sector 7 in Year 7, accompanied by a security team of four. Only his journal was recovered, found in a clearing where the shadows were too deep and the air tasted of copper and ozone.

His final entry:

> *"I see them watching me from the trees. Not attacking. Just... observing. I think they're curious. I think they want to know if I'll change. If I'll become like them."*

> *"The Void is not empty. The Void is **full**. Full of things that want to be real, that want to exist in coherent space and time. The Shadow Stalkers are the ones that made it. That pushed through the static and became... this."*

> *"I can feel it now. The invitation. The Void is offering. I could be faster. Stronger. I could see everything, know everything, be everywhere the darkness touches."*

> *"I'm going to accept. Someone needs to understand. Someone needs to see what's on the other side of the shadows."*

> *"If you're reading this... I'm sorry. And I'm not. You'll understand someday. Or you won't. Either way, I'll be waiting."*

"In the dark."

## Why They Drop PILLS

Shadow Stalker PILLS are the most valuable and the most dangerous. They contain concentrated Void-corruptionâ€”crystallized paradox, solidified impossibility.

Consuming one produces:

- **Void-sight** (the ability to perceive the Shadow Stalkers' "future memories")
- **Addiction** (the human mind was not meant to process acausal information)

Kermit refuses to handle Shadow Stalker PILLS. He won't explain why, but his hands shake when they're mentioned.

> *"Some things shouldn't be solid,*" he mutters. "*Some things shouldn't be consumed. The Bog Hopper PILLS are memories. The Shadow Stalker PILLS are **possibilities**. Futures that didn't happen. Futures that might. You're eating time itself, and time doesn't like being digested."*

He claims that people who take too many Shadow Stalker PILLS start to... fade. To become less certain. Their past becomes negotiable. Their future becomes fixed. They become more like the Stalkers themselvesâ€”creatures of probability rather than substance.

> *"I've seen it happen,*" he says, voice barely audible. "*People who took the PILLS and then... stopped being people. They still walk around. Still talk. But they're not there anymore. ## Current Status

The Shadow Stalker population is unknown. Estimates range from 50 to 500 individuals, based on sighting frequency and territorial requirements. They control all of Sector 7 and have been documented as far as Sector 4 during periods of Signal instability.

They do not breed. New Stalkers appear to be created through Void-corruption of other mammalian speciesâ€”feral cats, rats, one confirmed instance involving a researcher who ventured too close to the border.

The pack that emerged in Year 2 still exists. They are larger now. Smarter. More patient.

They wait in the dark, hunting futures where they win.

And they are winning more often.
